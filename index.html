About Us

We Provide

Welcome to Saitours, your gateway to unforgettable travel experiences in the beautiful island nation of Sri Lanka! Situated in the Indian Ocean, Sri Lanka is a tropical paradise renowned for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality.

Explore the misty plains of Horton Plains National Park, where you'll encounter breathtaking viewpoints such as World's End, offering panoramic vistas of valleys and mountains. Don't miss the vibrant Kandy Esala Perahera festival, a dazzling procession featuring beautifully adorned elephants, traditional dancers, musicians, and fire performers. And for an unforgettable encounter, visit the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage, where you can witness gentle giants up close and even participate in their care.

    Yala National Park

    Yala National Park in Sri Lanka is a renowned destination for wildlife enthusiasts. Spanning 979 square kilometers, it boasts a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and crocodiles. The park's rich biodiversity extends to over 200 bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. Its varied ecosystem comprises forests, grasslands, and lagoons, providing a unique and picturesque landscape for visitors to explore

    Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

    The Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage in Sri Lanka is a sanctuary and breeding ground for orphaned, abandoned, and injured elephants. Established in 1975, it is home to a large number of elephants, offering visitors a chance to observe and interact with these gentle giants up close. The orphanage also focuses on conservation efforts and elephant welfare, providing veterinary care and rehabilitation. Visitors can witness daily bathing and feeding sessions, and even volunteer to assist in caring for the elephants.

    Colombo - Capital Of Sri Lanka

    Colombo is the bustling capital city of Sri Lanka. It serves as the country's economic, cultural, and political hub. Located on the west coast, Colombo is a vibrant metropolis with a rich history and modern infrastructure. It offers a mix of colonial-era buildings, religious sites, shopping malls, and bustling markets. Key attractions include the Gangaramaya Temple, Galle Face Green, the National Museum, and the vibrant Pettah neighborhood. Colombo is also known for its diverse culinary scene, ranging from street food to fine dining options.

    Jami Ul-Alfar Mosque

    ami Ul-Alfar Mosque, also known as the Red Mosque, is a prominent landmark in Colombo, Sri Lanka. Located in the Pettah neighborhood, it is one of the oldest mosques in the city, dating back to 1908. The mosque's striking red and white striped exterior, along with its unique architecture, make it a distinctive sight. Jami Ul-Alfar Mosque is an important religious and cultural center for the Muslim community in Colombo. Visitors can appreciate its impressive facade and explore its peaceful interior adorned with intricate geometric patterns and calligraphy.

    Kandy - Historical Place

    Kandy is a historically significant city in Sri Lanka, known for its rich cultural heritage. It served as the last capital of the ancient kings of Sri Lanka and is home to several prominent historical and religious sites. The most iconic landmark is the Temple of the Tooth, which houses the sacred tooth relic of Lord Buddha. The Esala Perahera, a grand annual procession, takes place in Kandy, attracting thousands of visitors. The city's Royal Palace, now known as the National Museum of Kandy, offers insights into the country's royal history. Kandy's charming streets, colonial-era buildings, and serene lakes add to its historical charm.

    Ella - Secret Diamond In Sri Lanka

    Ella is often referred to as a "secret diamond" in Sri Lanka due to its breathtaking natural beauty. Nestled in the central highlands, this small town is surrounded by lush green tea plantations, mist-covered mountains, and cascading waterfalls. Ella is renowned for its stunning viewpoints, such as Ella Rock and Little Adam's Peak, which offer panoramic vistas of the picturesque landscape. The Nine Arch Bridge, an architectural marvel, is another popular attraction in the area.

    Peradeniya Botanical Garden

    Peradeniya Botanical Gardens is a renowned botanical paradise located near Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is one of the oldest and largest botanical gardens in the country, spanning over 147 acres. The garden was established in 1821 and is known for its diverse collection of plants, including rare orchids, palm trees, medicinal plants, and exotic flora from around the world. Visitors can explore the well-manicured lawns, beautiful flower beds, and themed gardens.
